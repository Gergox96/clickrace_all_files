Click Race!

Place your bets, let the races begin...

Prototype

The Click Race prototype has taken some directional shifts since the ideation phase of the project. Initially, 4 users were supposed to connect to the web page and select a colour.
The user would then become locked in and ONLY have access to clicking on their colour of choice. All 4 users would then click as fast as they can to race against the other opponents.

Here is a brief look at the system map that explains how each component of code and hardware work together to create a singular product, or in this case, merely a prototype.

    1 of 4 users click on a colour
    Sends data to Adafruit IO
    Adafruit sends data to Feather Huzzah
    Feather Huzzah rotates Servo Motors
    1 of 4 users shall win
    System Resets

Here are two screenshots from the prototype building process. The second screenshot is closer to the final version, but I wanted to run you through my thought process with this one.

Click Race was intended to be like the internet version of Hungry Hungry Hippos, all 4 users would be clicking like crazy until somebody wins, however, that idea changed through the process.

Here you can clearly see 4 buttons with no other options. Simply just click away at your colour and the code takes care of the rest.

In this version however, you'll quickly note that there is a new button labelled, "Erase Bet". I realised that there would most likely be complications and latency issues with 4 different users constantly clicking on buttons. So to solve this issue, I reshaped the idea of Click Race around race betting.

With the addition of bet placing, there is now an obvious, "Start" and "Stop" buttons. This simplifies the code to a boolean level where the circuit only has to thing,

"Okay, is the data a 1 or a 0? 1=start, 0=stop."
